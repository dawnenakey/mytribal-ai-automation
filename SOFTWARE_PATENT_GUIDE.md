## ü§î Can You Patent Software?

### Software Patent Requirements:

1. **Must Be Novel** (Never done before)
   - Your invention must be completely new
   - Not obvious improvement of existing tech
   - Must solve a problem uniquely

2. **Must Be Non-Obvious** 
   - A person skilled in the field wouldn't naturally think of it
   - Must be inventive, not just implementation

3. **Must Be Useful**
   - Must have practical application
   - Must actually work

4. **Must Be Patentable Subject Matter**
   - Pure algorithms often NOT patentable
   - Must have technical implementation
   - Must solve technical problem

### For Sign Language Recognition Software:

**Key Questions:**
- ‚ùì What's NEW about your approach?
- ‚ùì How is it different from existing systems?
- ‚ùì What technical innovation makes it patentable?
- ‚ùì Is it just applying AI to sign language (probably not patentable)?
- ‚ùì Or is there a novel technical method (might be patentable)?

## üìã Patent Process Overview

### Step 1: Patent Search (Critical)
**Cost: $500-$2,000**
- Search existing patents
- Find similar inventions
- Determine if your idea is novel
- **Hire patent attorney for this** - critical step

### Step 2: Provisional Patent Application (Optional but Recommended)
**Cost: $2,000-$5,000**
- Establishes priority date
- Gives you 1 year to file full patent
- Lower cost, buys you time
- **Good for software inventions**

### Step 3: Non-Provisional (Full) Patent Application
**Cost: $8,000-$20,000**
- Detailed technical documentation
- Claims (what you're actually patenting)
- Drawings and specifications
- **Must be very detailed and specific**

### Step 4: USPTO Review Process
**Time: 2-4 years**
- Examiner reviews application
- May reject multiple times
- You respond to objections
- Back-and-forth process
- **Additional costs: $2,000-$10,000+ for responses**

### Step 5: Patent Grant (If Approved)
**Ongoing: Maintenance Fees**
- Years 3.5, 7.5, 11.5: Pay maintenance fees ($1,600-$7,400 each)
- **If you don't pay, patent expires**

## üìã Patent Process - Step by Step

### Phase 1: Evaluation (Before Spending Money)

1. **Conduct Prior Art Search**
   - Google Patents search
   - USPTO patent database
   - Research papers
   - **Hire patent attorney** for professional search

2. **Evaluate Patentability**
   - Is it truly novel?
   - Is it non-obvious?
   - Does it meet technical requirements?
   - **Patent attorney will assess**

3. **Assess Business Value**
   - Is patent worth $30,000+?
   - Can you enforce it?
   - Will it prevent competitors?
   - ROI calculation

### Phase 2: Application (If You Proceed)

1. **Hire Patent Attorney**
   - Find attorney specializing in software/computer patents
   - Look for attorneys with AI/ML experience
   - Get quotes from 2-3 attorneys
   - **Cost: $300-$800/hour**

2. **File Provisional Patent**
   - Establishes priority date
   - Gives you 1 year
   - Lower cost entry point
   - **Must file within 1 year of public disclosure**

3. **Develop Detailed Specifications**
   - Technical details of invention
   - How it works
   - Algorithms, methods, systems
   - Code examples, flowcharts
   - **Patent attorney will guide you**

4. **File Non-Provisional Patent**
   - Detailed application
   - Claims (what you're patenting)
   - Detailed descriptions
   - Drawings/diagrams
   - **This is the expensive part**

### Phase 3: Prosecution (USPTO Review)

1. **Wait for Examination** (12-18 months)
2. **Respond to Office Actions** (likely multiple rounds)
3. **Amend Claims** as needed
4. **Final Decision** (2-4 years total)
